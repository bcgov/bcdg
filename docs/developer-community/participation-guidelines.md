# Developer Community participation guidelines

## Purpose

The Developer Community Microsoft Teams space is intended to support collaboration, knowledge sharing and respectful discussion among members of the B.C. government developer community.   

These participation guidelines support an inclusive, productive and professional environment across all community channels and align with the [B.C. Public Service Standards of Conduct](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/careers-myhr/about-the-bc-public-service/ethics-standards-of-conduct/standards-of-conduct). 

The guidelines apply to: 

* All members of the Developer Community MS Teams team 
* All channels, conversations, files and shared content 
* All interactions including posts, relies, tagging and file sharing 

## Community expectations

Members are expected to: 

1. **Act with integrity**

  * Be honest, accurate and professional
  * Share reliable information and correct mistakes when needed
  * Avoid conflicts of interest or situations that could affect objectivity 
  * Protect confidential, sensitive or restricted information 
  * Follow all applicable B.C. Public Service policies, standards and security requirements 

2. **Treat others fairly and with respect**

    * Use respectful, inclusive and accessible language
    * Always strive to consider the potential effects of our behaviour 
    * Respect different perspectives and experiences
    * Assume positive intent during discussions and collaboration 
    * Support a safe environment free from harassment or discrimination 

3. **Support the public interest** 

    * Keep discussions focused on improving services and shared technology 
    * Use channels for their intended purpose 
    * Contribute in ways that build trust 
    * Always follow the [Ethics and Standards of Conduct in the BC Public Service](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/careers-myhr/about-the-bc-public-service/ethics-standards-of-conduct)

## Communication guidelines

### Expected tone

The communication in channels should be: 

* Professional 
* Clear and concise 
* Respectful 
* Collaborative 
* Supportive and constructive 

Healthy discussion and technical debate are encouraged. Disagreements should focus on ideas, approaches, or technical decisions, not individuals.  

### When posting messages

Before posting: 

* Review previous messages to avoid duplicate questions
* Use appropriate channel for the topic 
* Search existing answers or resources where possible
* Consider whether your message benefits the broader community

When posting:

* Where possible use descriptive subject lines or opening statements
* Provide context and examples when asking technical questions (when, what, where)
* Tag people only when necessary
* Avoid excessive notifications or repeated tagging
* When possible use threads to keep conversations organized

### Acceptable behaviour 

Examples of acceptable behaviour include: 

* Helping others solve problems 
* Sharing knowledge, training resources, tools and best practices
* Asking respectful questions
* Providing constructive feedback
* Supporting inclusive participation
* Reporting issues appropriately
* Collaborating across channels respectfully  

### Unacceptable behaviour 

The following behaviours are not permitted:

* Harassment, bullying or intimidation
* Discrimination or hateful language
* Personal attacks or hostile behaviour
* Repeated disruptive or off-topic posting
* Spamming or excessive self-promotion
* Sharing inappropriate, explicit or offensive content
* Deliberately spreading misinformation
* Posting confidential or sensitive information
* Impersonation or misrepresentation
* Excessive tagging or notification abuse

## File sharing and content

Always follow [B.C. government information security and records management guidelines](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/governments/services-for-government/information-management-technology/information-management-in-cloud-based-tools#responsibilities). 

Members may share:

* Documentation
* Code snippets
* Technical resources
* Learning materials
* Links to approved tools or services 

Members must not share:

* Sensitive or confidential information
* Credentials, passwords or secrets
* Personal information without consent
* Copyrighted material without authorization
* Offensive, discriminatory or inappropriate content 

## Moderation and community management

### Channel moderation responsibilities for service or product teams

Service teams are responsible for helping maintain a respectful, inclusive and productive community environment. Their responsibilities include: 

* Supporting a respectful and productive environment
* Monitoring channels for inappropriate behaviour or content
* Guiding discussions back on topic when needed
* Responding to reported concerns
* Escalating issues when appropriate
* Applying moderation actions consistently and fairly 

Depending on the situation service teams may:

* Request the Developer Experience team to remove inappropriate messages or files
* Requests edits or clarification
* Redirect conversations to more appropriate channels
* Temporarily limit participation when necessary
* Escalate serious concerns to leadership, HR or security teams

### Channel moderation settings

Some channels may use Microsoft Teams [channel moderation settings](https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/office/change-moderator-roles-and-settings-in-a-channel-in-microsoft-teams-6ac54758-3440-4f6a-9bd5-cebf97cb7ea6) to support specific use cases, such as alerts, announcements or information-only channels. 

Channel moderation settings are a technical feature that controls who can create posts, reply or manage certain channel permissions.  

Examples may include: 

* Restricting who can start new posts 
* Limiting replies within a channel 
* Assigning channel moderators 
* Creating announcement or read-only style communication in channels 

!!! info Important 
    These channel moderation settings focus on the actual configuration and permissions. 
    
    Community moderation and management focus on maintaining a respectful, inclusive and productive environment through communication guidance, support and issue resolution. 

Requests to change channel settings for moderator roles should be directed to the Developer Experience (DevX) or if you are [requesting a new Developer Community channel](https://citz-do.atlassian.net/servicedesk/customer/portal/2/group/9/create/528) you can indicate to turn this feature on. 

## Reporting concerns and escalation process

If you encounter inappropriate behaviour or content in any of the channels:

1. Do not engage in hostile or escalating responses 
2. Report the issue to a moderator, Product Owner or Technical Lead 
3. Provide screenshots or message links where possible
4. Escalate urgent concerns involving safety, bullying, harassment or security immediately as per guidance by B.C. government: [Address discrimination, bullying and harassment](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/careers-myhr/all-employees/working-with-others/address-a-respectful-workplace-issue/address-discrimination-bulling-and-harassment)

## Channel usage guidance  

Channels should be used according to their intended purpose.  

Examples:
* [Service channels](https://bcgov.sharepoint.com/teams/Developercommunity/SitePages/All-channels.aspx?e=ZV4259) for questions, troubleshooting, important updates
* [Non-service channels](https://bcgov.sharepoint.com/teams/Developercommunity/SitePages/Non-service-channels.aspx) for discussion, building connection and knowledge sharing 

Additional guidance on using the Developer Community Microsoft Teams team, including channel structure, naming convention, how to submit a new channel and more, can be found in the [Developer Community Microsoft Teams guide](../developer-community/developer-community-ms-teams-guide.md).  Members are encouraged to review this guide to better understand how channels are organized and used within the community.

## External tools and integrations 

Only approved tools, bots and integrations should be using within the community space. Members should follow organizational security and procurement requirements before introducing new tools or services. [Get in touch with the Developer Experience (DevX)](https://citz-do.atlassian.net/servicedesk/customer/portal/2) for further guidance.  

## Final notes on participation 

Participation in the Developer Community Microsoft Teams team indicates agreement to follow these guidelines and all applicable [B.C. Public Service policies and standards](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/careers-myhr/about-the-bc-public-service/ethics-standards-of-conduct/standards-of-conduct). 

These guidelines may be updated over time to support evolving community needs and usage.
