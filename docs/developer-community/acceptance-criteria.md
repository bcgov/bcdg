# Acceptance criteria for public channels in the Developer Community

## Purpose

The Developer Experience (DevX) team manages the Microsoft Teams Developer Community and is responsible for ensuring that public channels remain purposeful, discoverable and aligned with community goals.

This internal process establishes:

* Clear acceptance criteria for approving or declining requests for new public channels
* A consistent review and decision workflow
* Guidance DevX can provide to requestors when a request does not meet the criteria

### Scope

This process applies to:

* Requests for new public channels within the [Developer Community Microsoft Teams](https://teams.microsoft.com/l/team/19%3A6bffce0ac7aa47a1ba9f6d9a7e898db9%40thread.tacv2/conversations?groupId=a80418da-c27b-406e-89ab-7695b61924d8&tenantId=6fdb5200-3d0d-4a8a-b036-d3685e359adc) space

It does not apply to:

* Private team or project channels
* One‑time events or short‑term initiatives

## Acceptance criteria

A request for a new public channel must meet all of the following criteria to be approved.

**Purpose**

* The channel has a clearly defined, ongoing purpose
* The purpose supports developer collaboration, learning, standards or shared services
* The request is not limited to a temporary project or engagement

**Audience**

* The channel is intended for a broad developer audience
* Content is appropriate for cross‑ministry or cross‑team visibility
* The channel is not limited to a single team, vendor, or working group.

**Alignment with Developer Community goals**

The channel contributes to one or more of the following:

* Knowledge sharing
* Best practices and standards
* Reusable solutions
* Developer support or enablement
* The topic aligns with the Developer Community’s purpose and tone

**Overlap with existing channels**

No significant duplication of existing public channels.

Where overlap exists:

* Justification is provided for why an existing channel cannot be used
* Consolidation has been considered

**Moderation** 

A service team, product owner and technical lead are identified.

The service teams agrees to:

* [Follow Service team channel guidelines](../developer-community/developer-community-ms-teams-guide.md#how-the-developer-community-is-structured)

## DevX decision process

**Step 1: Intake and review**

* DevX reviews the request against the acceptance criteria
    * JSM ticket asks if the request meets all the criteria. DevX ensure all the requirements have been met
* Clarification may be requested from the submitter if information is incomplete
* DevX may require edits on the channel name and description etc.

**Step 2: Decision**

* Approved
    * Channel creation proceeds
    * Ownership and moderation expectations are confirmed
* Declined
    * DevX documents the reason(s) for the decision
    * DevX provides clear next‑step guidance

### Guidance when a request is declined

When a request does not meet approval criteria, DevX will recommend one or more alternatives, such as:

* Creating a Microsoft Teams channel for team‑specific or short-term need outside of the Developer Community Microsoft Teams team 
* Using an existing public Developer Community channel
* Publishing content through other approved platforms (e.g., DevHub, documentation, announcements)

Where possible, links or instructions will be provided to support the requestor.
